The data story I found for this week is a Pew Research Center article about aging in America and how people of lower income families are more likely to be caregivers to their aging parents or spouses. Here is a link to the story: https://www.pewresearch.org/social-trends/2026/02/26/family-caregiving-in-an-aging-america/ 
The article has a few good visualizations of the survey data, but the one that I think is the most interesting is one that compares whether they think regularly caring for an aging parent positively or negatively affects different parts of their lives, split by gender. Women more than men say that caring for an aging parent negatively impacts their emotional wellbeing and physical health. I find this interesting because women are traditionally seen as caregivers and have that expectation that they will care more people. I wonder how these responses would have changed over time as women shift away from traditional caregiver roles.

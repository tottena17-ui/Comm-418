Another data story I found is from ProPublica. This story talks about the DOJ dropped 23,000 criminal cases to focus on immigration. The story does not have visuals, but it is great at discussing data and making the data digustable and impactful to a reader. The article talks about the types of cases being dropped, the scope of their analysis, and historical comparisons to get the depth of the situation. This article is a great example of how to make data the center of the story and impactful to the reader, without throwing a lot of numbers at the readers. Bringing the data into context is just as important to the story as the data itself. Here is a link to the article: https://www.propublica.org/article/trump-doj-immigration-bondi-declinations-criminal-investigations

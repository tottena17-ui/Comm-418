My data story for this week is from ESPN. Instead of graphs or visuals it contains tables of information. The story is about a new tracking system that the MLB is implementing for the 2026 season. The ABS challenge tracking system allows players to challenge whether the pitch was a ball or a strike. The tables show batters who have challenged the most pitches, catchers who have challenged the most pitches, and teams who have challenged the most pitches. This page will be continuously updated throughout the season. This is interesting because it can be the base for other stories throughout the baseball season. Is one team challenging a lot more than the rest? How many challenges do catchers average versus batters? Since this is the first year of its implementation, this data is also important to not only fans but the league to determine whether the system is effective in the MLB. It is not only a story about what the new system is, but it is a reference point that fans, journalists, and other interested stakeholders can refer back to.
